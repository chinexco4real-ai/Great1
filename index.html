Dual login auto-switch
Alternates between two tokens. Login 1 runs until it hits its loss limit or loss-streak, then that market's session resets and switches to Login 2. Login 2 runs until it hits its profit target or timer, then switches back to Login 1 — back and forth, non-stop. With multi-market on, every selected market does this independently on its own counters — one market switching never affects any other.

● SCANNING
Select two or more markets to scan simultaneously. Auto-trading, stake, duration, strength and cooldown settings above are shared rules applied to all selected markets. The Daily loss limit and Daily profit target fields above apply to the primary "Market" panel only — multi-market instances use the two dedicated targets below instead, so the two systems never compete over the same market. If dual-login auto-switch is also on, each market independently tracks its own condition 1 / condition 2 counters and switches to Login 2 on its own — see the note below.
Both are universal values applied to every market in the scan, and each only ever applies while that market is on its own stated login — a market is never on both logins at once, so the two never conflict. Reset target (Login 1): the moment a market's own net P&L reaches this amount while it's on Login 1, that market's stats snap back to 0 and it keeps trading. Closing target (Login 2): the moment a market's own net P&L reaches this amount while it's on Login 2, that market closes out and is disabled — it stops trading and drops out of the scan, while every other market keeps running independently until it reaches its own target. Once every selected market has closed out, the scan stops itself. Set either to 0 to disable it.
Dual-login is active — each market in this scan switches to Login 2 independently, the moment its OWN condition 1 or condition 2 is met (not combined with any other market's count). Each market then runs its own Login 2 profit target and timer, and banks its own vault snapshot when that leg ends, all without affecting any other market. A market only waits for its own open trade to settle before switching — other markets are never held up by it.
