Dual login auto-switch
Alternates between two tokens. Login 1 runs until it hits its loss limit or loss-streak, then that market's session resets and switches to Login 2. Login 2 runs until it hits its profit target or timer, then switches back to Login 1 — back and forth, non-stop. With multi-market on, every selected market does this independently on its own counters — one market switching never affects any other.

Login 1 → Login 2 reverse mode
Per-market, not global: while this is on, every selected market trades normal direction for as long as it's on Login 1, and automatically flips to reverse direction the moment it switches to Login 2 — independently, based on each market's own current login, not a shared switch. One market can be on Login 1 (normal) while another is already on Login 2 (reverse) at the same time. The main Reverse mode toggle on the right is disabled while this is on, since it isn't needed here — each market's reverse behaviour is driven entirely by its own login state instead.

Auto-trade controls
Auto-trading
Places Rise/Fall trades automatically on qualifying signals
Reverse mode
Flips every trade to the opposite direction right before it's placed — a Rise signal executes as Fall (PUT), and a Fall signal executes as Rise (CALL). The signal panel keeps showing the real detected direction; only the trade that's actually sent, logged, and settled is reversed.
Off: signals fire exactly as originally specified — Momentum, CCI, Stoch RSI and MACD only, no trend bias. When enabled, a 5th gate is added: a trade can only fire in the direction of the prevailing trend, so the EA never trades against it.

Trade log
Auto-clear log
Every 10 minutes, checks this log's total entries — closed & settled trades (won/lost — never an open position) plus system-log entries, counted together as one number. The moment that combined total reaches 50, the entire log is cleared. If it's still under 50, it waits another 10 minutes and checks again.
