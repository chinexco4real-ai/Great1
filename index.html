Dual login auto-switch
Alternates between two tokens. Login 1 runs until it hits its loss limit, then the EA resets its session and switches to Login 2. Login 2 runs until it hits its profit target, then switches back to Login 1 — back and forth, non-stop.
Login 1 → Login 2 switch conditions
Turn on any combination. If more than one is on, whichever condition is met first triggers the switch.
Condition 1 — net loss limit
Switch once Login 1's net P&L drops to −the amount below.
Loss limit, in USD.
Condition 2 — consecutive losses
Switch once this many losing trades happen in a row on Login 1.
Straight losing trades, count.
Condition 3 — consecutive wins
Switch once this many winning trades happen in a row on Login 1. Before switching, the EA turns reverse mode on; when Login 2 later hits its profit target and switches back, reverse mode is automatically turned back off. While this condition is on, dual mode owns reverse mode for the cycle — manually toggling it won't stick.
Straight winning trades, count.
While Login 2 is active, switch back to Login 1 once net P&L reaches +this amount.
Login 2 timer close
Starts a countdown the moment Login 2 becomes active. Login 2 switches back to Login 1 on whichever happens first: the profit target above, or this countdown running out.
If the profit target hasn't been reached when this runs out, the EA attempts to close any open trade and switches back to Login 1.

Auto-trade controls
Auto-trading

Alternator mode
Forces strict BUY → SELL → BUY → SELL alternation. A repeat-direction signal is blocked no matter how strong or how many filters agree — the EA waits for the opposite direction. Applies on top of every signal and filter.
Next required direction: either (no trade yet this session)
Reverse mode
Flips the final decided direction right before placing the trade — a would-be BUY is placed as SELL, and a would-be SELL as BUY. Applied last, after every signal, filter, and the alternator gate.
